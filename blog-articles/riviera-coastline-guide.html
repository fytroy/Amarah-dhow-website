Guide to Exploring the Riviera Coastline by Sail

By Sarah Miller | July 10, 2025 | Back to Blog

The Riviera coastline, with its sun-drenched shores, charming fishing villages, and dramatic cliffs, is a dream destination for sailors. Discover how to explore this stunning region by sail, finding hidden coves and enjoying exquisite local cuisine.

Sailing the Azure Waters

The azure waters of the Riviera offer excellent sailing conditions, particularly during the spring and autumn months when the crowds are thinner and the winds are gentle. Navigate past opulent villas, bustling marinas, and serene natural reserves.

Must-Visit Anchorages

  • Portofino Bay: A picturesque harbor town, perfect for a short stop and a stroll through its colorful streets.
  • Calanques National Park: Between Marseille and Cassis, this area boasts stunning limestone cliffs and turquoise coves accessible only by boat.
  • Lerins Islands (near Cannes): Offers sheltered anchorages and beautiful walking trails, including the historic Sainte-Marguerite Island.

Local Culinary Delights

No trip to the Riviera is complete without indulging in its culinary treasures. Many coastal restaurants offer moorings for smaller boats, allowing you to dine with a view of your vessel.

Practical Tips for Your Voyage

Consider the wind patterns; the Mistral can be strong, especially in the western parts. Always check weather forecasts. Moorings can be limited in peak season, so plan your stops or book ahead. Enjoy the slower pace of life and let the coastal breezes guide your unforgettable journey!
