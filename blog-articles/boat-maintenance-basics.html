Boat Maintenance Basics: Keeping Your Vessel Shipshape

By Maintenance Crew | June 28, 2025 | Back to Blog

Owning or chartering a boat comes with the responsibility of maintaining it to ensure safety, longevity, and optimal performance. Regular maintenance prevents costly repairs and ensures smooth sailing. Here are some basic boat maintenance tips every boat enthusiast should know.

1. Regular Cleaning and Washing

Saltwater and sun can be harsh on your boat's exterior. Rinse your boat with fresh water after every outing to remove salt residue. Regularly wash the deck and hull with boat-friendly soap to prevent buildup of grime, algae, and mildew. Pay attention to crevices and non-skid surfaces.

2. Engine Checks (Pre- and Post-Trip)

Your engine is the heart of your boat. Before each trip, check fluid levels (oil, coolant, power steering fluid), fuel lines for leaks, and belts for tension. After your trip, flush the engine with fresh water if you've been in saltwater. Follow your engine's manual for recommended service intervals, including oil changes and filter replacements.

3. Inspecting Hoses, Belts, and Wiring

Periodically inspect all hoses for cracks, bulges, or leaks. Check belts for proper tension and signs of fraying. Look for any exposed or corroded wiring. Addressing these small issues early can prevent major breakdowns on the water. Keep spare belts and common hose clamps onboard.

4. Battery Care

Batteries are crucial for starting your engine and powering electronics. Keep battery terminals clean and free of corrosion. Ensure they are fully charged before each trip, especially if your boat has been sitting for a while. Consider a trickle charger for long-term storage.

5. Check Your Safety Equipment

Before every trip, quickly verify that all safety equipment is in good working order and easily accessible. This includes life jackets, flares (check expiry dates!), fire extinguishers, first-aid kits, and the bilge pump. A quick check can save lives.

By incorporating these basic maintenance practices into your routine, you'll ensure your vessel remains shipshape and ready for countless enjoyable adventures on the water.
